# MindShift — Brand Guidelines

Foundation for all branding decisions: logo, landing page, README, UI, and marketing. MindShift is an AI-powered web game that teaches cognitive biases through interactive decision-making. The brand should feel **premium, modern, and memorable** — intelligent, approachable, and fun. Never childish. Never dry or academic.

---

## Brand Vision

A world where clear thinking is a trainable skill, and sharpening your judgment feels like play.

## Brand Mission

Turn cognitive-bias education into a game people genuinely want to play — so better thinking happens through action, not lecture.

## Brand Personality

Think of MindShift as a **sharp, witty mentor** who respects your intelligence.

- **Smart** — grounded in real science, never condescending.
- **Playful** — confident enough to be fun about serious ideas.
- **Encouraging** — celebrates growth, never shames mistakes.
- **Clean & confident** — calm, uncluttered, self-assured.
- **Curious** — invites exploration and "aha" moments.

## Core Values

- **Clarity** — make complex thinking simple and usable.
- **Growth** — every session leaves the player sharper.
- **Honesty** — science first; no manipulation or dark patterns.
- **Delight** — quality and craft in every detail.
- **Respect** — treat players as capable adults.

## Target Audience

Curious learners, students, professionals, and self-improvers (roughly 16+) who want sharper decision-making. Global, mobile-first. They value quality products, enjoy learning, and are drawn to intelligent design — not gamified fluff.

## Tone of Voice

- Clear, concise, confident.
- Warm and human — write like a smart friend, not a textbook.
- Light wit where it fits; never sarcastic at the player's expense.
- Explain the *why* simply; skip jargon unless earned.
- Second person ("you"). Active voice. Short sentences.

**Yes:** "You just fell for anchoring — and almost everyone does. Here's how to spot it next time."
**No:** "The anchoring heuristic represents a systematic deviation from rational judgment as described in the literature."

## Brand Keywords

Intelligent · Modern · Playful · Premium · Clean · Insightful · Empowering · Sharp · Approachable · Memorable

## Emotional Experience

The player should feel: **curious → challenged → surprised → enlightened → accomplished.** Above all, "that was fun *and* I learned something about how my mind works." Never confused, patronized, or bored.

## Design Philosophy

Design serves clarity and delight. Every element earns its place. Premium SaaS quality — spacious, minimal, intentional. Motion and visuals guide understanding; they never decorate for their own sake.

## Visual Style

- Clean, minimal, spacious. Generous whitespace.
- Modern and confident — flat-forward with subtle depth (soft shadows, gentle layering), not heavy skeuomorphism.
- Strong typographic hierarchy carries the interface.
- Focused compositions — one clear action per screen.
- Consistent, systematic spacing and rhythm.

## Color Palette Direction

_(Direction only — no hex yet.)_

- One confident, intelligent primary — cool and modern (think deep blue/indigo/violet family), signaling trust and thought.
- A vivid accent for energy, rewards, and key actions — used sparingly for punch.
- A calm, near-neutral base (soft off-whites and refined grays) so content breathes.
- Semantic support colors for success / caution / error, tuned to feel gentle, not alarming.
- Full light and dark themes, both first-class.
- Restraint over rainbow — a tight, purposeful palette.

## Typography Direction

- Modern geometric or humanist **sans-serif** for UI and body — highly readable, clean, contemporary.
- A characterful display face (or expressive weight of the primary family) for headlines and moments of personality.
- Clear type scale with strong hierarchy; comfortable line length and spacing.
- Optional monospace for data, stats, or "system" moments.
- Legibility first — especially on mobile.

## Iconography Style

- Consistent line-based icon set, medium weight, rounded terminals to feel approachable.
- Simple, geometric, instantly legible at small sizes.
- One system throughout — no mixed styles.
- Icons clarify meaning; they never merely decorate.

## Illustration Style

- Light, modern, conceptual — visualize thinking, choices, and "mind" metaphors abstractly.
- Flat with subtle gradients/depth; on-palette.
- Friendly and smart, never cartoonish or clip-art.
- Used purposefully — onboarding, empty states, achievements, key narrative beats.

## Animation Philosophy

- Purposeful motion that guides attention and rewards progress.
- Smooth, quick, natural easing — responsive, never sluggish.
- Micro-interactions on feedback moments (correct/incorrect, XP, unlocks) to make learning tangible.
- Subtle by default; celebratory only at genuine milestones.
- Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Performance over spectacle.

## Logo Direction

- Concept: a **shift** in thinking — transformation, perspective change, the "click" of insight.
- Explore a wordmark with one subtle twist (a rotated/mirrored letter, a directional cue) suggesting a mental pivot.
- Symbol ideas: an abstract mind/brain, a shifting or rotating shape, a spark/lightbulb reimagined minimally, arrows or a pivot mark.
- Must work as a small favicon/app icon and scale up cleanly.
- Clean, geometric, memorable, timeless — no gradients-as-crutch, no literal cartoon brain.

## UI Style Inspiration

Reference the polish of modern premium products: Linear (clean, fast, focused), Duolingo (playful learning loop, rewarding feedback — without the childishness), Notion (calm clarity), Arc/Raycast (confident modern craft), Apple (restraint and hierarchy). Blend Linear's precision with Duolingo's rewarding progression, kept adult and premium.

## Things the Brand Should Avoid

- Childish, cartoonish, or "gamified toy" aesthetics.
- Dry, academic, textbook tone.
- Visual clutter, busy layouts, competing focal points.
- Excessive gradients, random colors, neon overload.
- Inconsistent spacing, mixed icon/illustration styles.
- Over-animation and gratuitous effects.
- Dark patterns, manipulative streak-guilt, or engagement bait.
- Stocky clip-art, literal brain clichés, generic templated looks.
